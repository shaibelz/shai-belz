Addressing the Concern: "Merchants Need Offline for Non-Digital Transactions"

Valid concern. Some merchants enable offline to document cash, checks, or bank transfers for accounting. Here's what the data shows:

Mobile Sessions Where Offline is ShownSessions% of TotalGPV
Did NOT click/pay offline6.05M94%$412M online GPV
Actually paid via offline384K6%$56M offline GPV

Our recommendation: Deprioritize (not hide) offline on mobile. Show it collapsed / below the fold. Keep it fully available on desktop. Merchant can always override. This protects the 6% who need it while removing friction for the 94% who don't.

Hypothesis: Showing "offline" on a mobile screen creates decision friction -- buyers wonder if they should pay now or later. Only 6% of mobile buyers who see offline actually use it. The conversion gap is 9-20pp across verticals, suggesting the display itself creates hesitation for the other 94%.

Methodology & Statistical Rigor

How we calculated the GPV estimates, what "statistically significant" means for this study, and what the limitations are.

1. Data Source

We used prod.payments.checkout_session_log -- a session-level table recording every checkout in 2025. Each row is one checkout session with the exact payment methods displayed, the buyer's country, device, vertical, and whether the order was paid digitally.

MetricValue
Date rangeJan 1 - Dec 31, 2025
Total sessions189.5M (with regular checkout shown)
Mobile sessions59.4M
Desktop sessions20.6M
Conversion metricis_regular_checkout_order_paid = digitally completed payment

Important: The conversion metric tracks digitally completed payments. Offline payments (cash, bank transfer) that complete outside the checkout flow may not be captured. This is noted explicitly in Finding #2.

2. Statistical Test: Chi-Square (χ²) Test of Independence

For each finding, we compare two groups (e.g., "1 BNPL shown" vs "2+ BNPLs shown") using a 2×2 contingency table:

PaidNot Paid
Config Aab
Config Bcd

The χ² test measures whether the conversion difference is statistically significant -- i.e., not due to random chance.

Why Chi-Square and not Fisher's Exact? Fisher's Exact test is ideal for small samples (<1,000). With millions of sessions, Chi-Square is appropriate and computationally feasible. Both give equivalent conclusions at our sample sizes.

4. How to Read These Results

Strong findings (effect size > 0.05)
  • BNPL capping: 5.75 pp gap, χ²=8,868, highly significant
  • Offline hiding: 9.6-17.8 pp gap, χ²=93K-149K, very strong signal
  • Germany PayPal: 21.2 pp gap, V=0.216, strongest effect
  • Events BNPLs: 16.5 pp gap, χ²=20,461, very strong
Weaker findings (effect size < 0.05)
  • Mobile 3→4 methods: 0.4 pp gap, V=0.004 -- statistically significant but the effect is small
  • Desktop 3→5 methods: 0.1 pp gap, NOT significant (p=0.19). Desktop conversion doesn't meaningfully change from 3 to 5 methods

These smaller effects are still directionally useful -- they confirm the mobile/desktop divergence pattern even if each individual step is modest.

5. Correlation vs Causation: The Key Limitation

This is an observational study, not a randomized A/B test. The conversion gaps are statistically significant -- they are real patterns in the data, not noise. But we cannot claim with certainty that the payment method config caused the entire gap.

What we controlled for
  • Device type (Mobile / Desktop)
  • Vertical (Stores / Bookings / Events / etc.)
  • Country (US / DE / AU / FR / UK)
  • Payment method baseline (CC present in both groups)
What we could NOT control for
  • Merchant quality / site design
  • Product price range / category
  • Buyer intent / traffic source
  • Merchant tenure / experience
Bottom line: The observed gaps represent the upper bound of the opportunity. A/B tests will reveal what portion is causal. Given the large effect sizes and the number of confounders we did control for, we expect a substantial portion of the gap to hold in a controlled experiment.

6. How GPV Estimates Are Calculated

For each finding, the GPV estimate follows this formula:

GPV increase = Affected Sessions × Conversion Gap × Avg Order Value

Example: BNPL Capping (Finding #1)

ComponentValueSource
Affected sessions1.26MSessions showing 2+ BNPLs on US Mobile
Conversion gap5.75 pp72.5% (1 BNPL) minus 66.75% (2+ BNPLs)
Additional paid sessions~72,4501.26M × 5.75%
Avg order value~$113$142M GPV / 1.26M sessions (paid)
GPV increase~$8.2M72,450 × $113

The presented range ($17-20M) includes the broader effect across all US Mobile sessions where excess BNPLs are shown, not just the 2+ vs 1 comparison.

GPV estimates represent the full observed gap

The estimates apply the full observed conversion difference to the affected session pool. We do NOT apply an arbitrary discount. The gaps are statistically significant (p<0.001 for all major findings), meaning they reflect real patterns. The only caveat is causality -- A/B testing will determine what portion of the gap is directly caused by the payment method config vs other correlated factors.

Offline finding: accounting for offline GPV at risk

The offline GPV estimate is net of offline GPV at risk. Of 6.43M mobile sessions where offline is shown alongside CC: 94% never click offline (they pay online or abandon), and 6% actually pay offline ($56M GPV). If we deprioritize offline, some of those 6% may not complete their purchase. Assuming 25-50% of mobile offline payers are lost (the rest shift to online payment), we deduct $14-28M from the theoretical lift. This gives the $24-34M net estimate.

8. Recommended Statistical Next Steps

1
A/B Test the Top 2 Findings

Randomly assign mobile checkout sessions to show/hide offline (Stores) and cap/uncap BNPLs (US). 2-4 weeks of data will confirm the causal conversion lift.

2
Propensity Score Matching

Before A/B tests, we can strengthen the observational evidence by matching merchants in the "good config" group with similar merchants in the "bad config" group on observable characteristics (price range, traffic volume, vertical, country). This reduces confounding.

3
Monitor Effect Size, Not Just Significance

With millions of sessions, any tiny difference becomes "significant." Focus on Cramér's V and the conversion point difference. Our strongest findings (V>0.08, gaps >5pp) represent meaningful real-world effects.
