Multi Currency — Default ON

It's Time to Test the Real Default Experience

We ran a controlled experiment on ~400 Multi Currency sites. Early signals show significant conversion uplift in the mature cohort. But 237K visitors isn't enough to know for sure. The only way to validate the real impact is to go Default ON — and test at real scale.

02 — Early Signals
Significant Results in the Mature Cohort
The 1st batch (28 alpha sites, 42K+ visitors/group, 9 weeks of data) now shows significant results across multiple metrics. The 2nd and 3rd batches haven't reached significance yet — we need more scale.
1st Batch — Significant (42K+/group)
+9.2% Order Paid (sig) [+0.3%..+18.9%]
+12.9% Regular Checkout Paid (sig) [+2.0%..+23.8%]
+32.2% PayPal Regular Paid (sig) [+3.9%..+60.5%]
+8.7% Checkout Widget Order Placed (non-sig)

All Population — Not Yet Significant (123K/114K)

Batches 2–3 added recently, need more time
Express checkout flat
237K visitors on 400 sites — need millions to be confident
Stores funnel: +2.9% increase in gallery browsing (sig), but slight decreases in product page (−1.9pp) and add-to-cart (−1.5pp) step conversion — more visitors browse, but the extra browsers are more casual. Checkout and payment conversion remain clearly improved.
Visitors prefer their local currency: Only 1.18% of Group B visitors used the currency converter to switch back to the site currency after being auto-switched. Once visitors see prices in their own currency, they overwhelmingly stay. This reinforces the case for Default ON — auto-display aligns with what buyers actually want.
We can keep testing on 400 sites for months and never get statistical power on the overall metric. We need 10–50x that volume to get tight confidence intervals. The path to certainty isn't more waiting — it's more scale.
