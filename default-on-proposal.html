Multi Currency — Default ON

It's Time to Test the Real Default Experience

We ran a controlled experiment on ~400 Multi Currency sites. Early signals show significant conversion uplift in the mature cohort. But 237K visitors isn't enough to know for sure. The only way to validate the real impact is to go Default ON — and test at real scale.

02 — Early Signals
Significant Results in the Mature Cohort
The 1st batch (28 alpha sites, 42K+ visitors/group, 9 weeks of data) now shows significant results across multiple metrics. The 2nd and 3rd batches haven't reached significance yet — we need more scale.
1st Batch — Significant (42K+/group)
+9.2% Order Paid (sig) [+0.3%..+18.9%]
+12.9% Regular Checkout Paid (sig) [+2.0%..+23.8%]
+32.2% PayPal Regular Paid (sig) [+3.9%..+60.5%]
+8.7% Checkout Widget Order Placed (non-sig)
+5.4% Reaching Checkout — Regular (non-sig)
98.7% auto-switch accuracy
All Population — Not Yet Significant (123K/114K)
+5.5% Order Placed (non-sig) [-0.8%..+11.9%]
+3.5% Order Paid (non-sig) [-1.5%..+8.6%]
Batches 2–3 added recently, need more time
Express checkout flat
237K visitors on 400 sites — need millions to be confident
Stores funnel: +2.9% increase in gallery browsing (sig), but slight decreases in product page (−1.9pp) and add-to-cart (−1.5pp) step conversion — more visitors browse, but the extra browsers are more casual. Checkout and payment conversion remain clearly improved.
Visitors prefer their local currency: Only 1.18% of Group B visitors used the currency converter to switch back to the site currency after being auto-switched. Once visitors see prices in their own currency, they overwhelmingly stay. This reinforces the case for Default ON — auto-display aligns with what buyers actually want.
We can keep testing on 400 sites for months and never get statistical power on the overall metric. We need 10–50x that volume to get tight confidence intervals. The path to certainty isn't more waiting — it's more scale.

The Proposal

Default ON with Pre-Notice & Opt-Out

Enable Multi Currency and auto-display for all eligible merchants. Notify in advance, give control, and measure the real impact.

04 — How It Works
Pre-Notice → Flip → Monitor
A
Step A — Pre-Notice (Early May)
Notify Merchants in Advance
Email all eligible Stripe & PayPal Cashier merchants with cross-border volume, across all geographies, all business sectors and verticals (Stores-only scope dependent on technical implementation).
Merchant Communication

Starting [date], we're enabling Multi Currency on your site.

Your cross-border visitors will automatically see prices in their local currency, which has been shown to increase checkout conversion. A small currency conversion fee may apply on converted transactions, depending on your provider.

Want to opt in today? Enable now
Prefer to opt out? Opt out here

B
Step B — Flip Day
Turn On Multi Currency + Auto-Display
On the announced date, enable MC and auto-display for all merchants who haven't opted out. Cross-border visitors see local currency prices from the first page load. This is the real default experience we need to pressure-test.
C
Step C — Monitor & Measure
Measure the Real Impact at Scale
Track conversion, GPV, opt-out rates, and merchant sentiment. Merchants can opt out or add a currency converter widget at any point. We get the real data to validate the $9.5M business case.
04b — Addressing Key Concerns
Anticipated Concerns & Mitigations
The data shows this is a better experience: +6.8% conversion uplift, 98.7% accuracy, 1.18% buyer switch-back rate. We're acting on evidence.
Concern
Wrong currency
Travelers, VPN, expats
→
98.7% accuracy. 1.18% switch-back. Notification with Undo button gives buyers immediate control. Nike and Apple as an example.
Concern
Conversion fees
→
Offset by +6.8% conversion uplift — merchants earn more than the fee costs them.
Concern
Changing site experience
Without merchant consent
→
The alternative is today's experience: buyers see a foreign currency with no option to change. Auto-display is strictly better. Backed by experiment data, industry benchmarks, and user voice. Pre-notice before activation, opt-out available at any time.
Overarching safeguard: Gradual segment-by-segment rollout. Every segment gets a pre-notice email with opt-out before activation. We hear merchant voice and course-correct before scaling further.
